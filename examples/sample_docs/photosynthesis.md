# Photosynthesis: a brief overview

Photosynthesis is the biochemical process by which plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy — usually from the sun — into chemical energy stored in glucose. The overall reaction can be written as:

    6 CO2 + 6 H2O + light energy -> C6H12O6 + 6 O2

The process has two stages:

1. **Light-dependent reactions** occur in the thylakoid membranes. Chlorophyll absorbs photons, water is split (releasing O2), and the energy carriers ATP and NADPH are produced.

2. **The Calvin cycle** runs in the stroma. It uses ATP and NADPH from stage 1 to fix CO2 into G3P, which is then assembled into glucose. The key enzyme is RuBisCO, widely considered the most abundant protein on Earth.

Photosynthesis is the primary route by which carbon enters the biosphere, and its efficiency varies sharply by plant strategy: C3, C4, and CAM plants differ in how they handle high temperatures and water stress.
